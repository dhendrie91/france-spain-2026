Stop One · Basque Coast

San Sebastián

01
Thursday 6 — Saturday 8 August · 3 days, 2 nights
Lasala Plaza Hotel — Plaza de la Constitución · in the heart of the Parte Vieja
3 Days · 2 Nights · Lasala Plaza Hotel

Off the ferry on Thursday afternoon and an hour east along the Basque coast to one of Europe's great food cities. The hotel is bang in the middle of the Old Town — two minutes' walk from the harbour, La Concha beach, and the legendary pintxos bars.

A leisurely Saturday morning before driving on — Boulevard coffee, maybe one last pintxos lunch before pointing the car north into France.

Highlights
  • Pintxos crawl — Bar Néstor's txuleta & tomato salad, Bar Goiz Argi prawn brochettes, La Cuchara de San Telmo octopus
  • Sunset from Monte Igueldo over La Concha bay
  • Morning swim or stroll along Playa de la Concha
  • Dinner at a sit-down asador if we fancy a slow evening
Stop Two · Gascony

Les Prés d'Eugénie

02
Saturday 8 — Monday 10 August · 1 full day
Les Prés d'Eugénie — Relais & Châteaux · Empress Eugénie's old spa village
1 Full Day · Les Prés d'Eugénie

Two hours north from San Sebastián into the deep green countryside of the Landes. Michel Guérard's beloved Relais & Châteaux — a constellation of gardens, salons, three restaurants and a spa across the village.

Wine country at the doorstep: Madiran reds, Pacherenc whites, Armagnac just over the next hill.

Highlights
  • Dinner at the gastronomic restaurant — the Guérard legacy under Hugo Souchet
  • Lighter lunch at La Ferme aux Grives, the family bistro
  • Tasting at Plaimont (Saint-Mont) — pre-phylloxera vines, rare grape varieties
  • Optional Armagnac tour at Domaine Duffau in Eauze
  • Spa & pool afternoons
Stop Three · The Tarn

Château de Fiac

03
Monday 10 — Wednesday 12 August · 1 full day
Château de Fiac Hôtel & Spa — restored Languedoc château with infinity pool & celebrated restaurant
1 Full Day · Château de Fiac

East from Eugénie via lunch in Auch — the Gascon capital, duck-and-foie-gras country, with a UNESCO Gothic cathedral. Onwards into the rolling Tarn hills.

A quiet base with one of France's most extraordinary heritage cities, Albi, only thirty minutes north.

Highlights
  • Auch lunch en route — Hôtel de France or La Table d'Oste for proper Gascon cooking
  • Albi: the world's largest brick cathedral & the Musée Toulouse-Lautrec (1,000+ works)
  • Cordes-sur-Ciel — the medieval village in the sky
  • Afternoon spa & infinity pool back at the château
Stop Four · Medieval Cité

Carcassonne

04
Wednesday 12 — Friday 14 August · 2 days, 2 nights
Hôtel du Château & Spa — Teritoria · directly outside the walls of the UNESCO cité
2 Days · 2 Nights · Hôtel du Château

Only an hour from Fiac. The largest fortified medieval city in Europe — double curtain walls, fifty-two towers, better than any film set. Our hotel sits right outside, with breakfast on a terrace facing the fortress.

Best at dusk, when the day-trippers leave and the stone turns gold.

Highlights
  • Evening visit inside the Cité after the crowds leave
  • Cathar castle day: Peyrepertuse & Quéribus on the dramatic limestone ridges
  • Or the four castles of Lastours as a half-day
  • Cassoulet supper at a proper old-town restaurant
  • Spa afternoon before the drive to the coast
Stop Five · Mediterranean

Collioure

05
Friday 14 — Monday 17 August · 4 days, 3 nights
Hôtel La Casa Païral — hidden retreat in the old quarter · garden & pool
4 Days · 3 Nights · Hôtel La Casa Païral

Down to the sea. The Catalan fishing village Matisse and Derain made famous, where the Pyrenees tumble into the Mediterranean. Bell tower with its feet in the water, Royal Castle on the harbour, terracotta rooftops, pebble beaches, apéro on the quay as the sun goes down.

Three nights — long enough to settle in properly, and to celebrate the anniversary somewhere in the middle.

Highlights
  • Day trip into Spain: Cadaqués & Cap de Creus — whitewashed Catalan village, Dalí's home, wild headland
  • Alternative day trip: Peyrepertuse Cathar castle
  • Anniversary lunch on Sunday 16 August at La Balette · 12:30pm · Michelin-starred terrace over the bay
  • Apéro at Les Templiers or Casa Reial on the harbour
  • Swimming off the beach by the church · sunset behind the castle
Stop Six · Return Via Pamplona

Pamplona & Bilbao

06
Monday 17 — Thursday 20 August · 3 days, 3 nights
The Artist Grand Hotel of Art — directly opposite the Guggenheim · rooftop bar with museum views
3 Days · 3 Nights · The Artist Grand Hotel

West across northern Spain, breaking the long drive with a proper late lunch in Pamplona — a walk along the famous bull-run route, pintxos in Plaza del Castillo, the cathedral, and back on the road.

On to Bilbao for the last three nights. Our hotel sits directly opposite Frank Gehry's Guggenheim — rooftop looks straight at the titanium curves of the museum, riverside walk to the old town in fifteen minutes.

Highlights
  • Pamplona lunch — Bar Gaucho's Solomillo (once voted best pintxo in Spain) or Casa Otano
  • Guggenheim Bilbao — timed entry, ideally mid-morning for the atrium light
  • Pintxos in the Casco Viejo: Las Siete Calles, Plaza Nueva, Mercado de la Ribera
  • Day trip: San Juan de Gaztelugatxe — the chapel on the rocky island
  • Asador dinner — traditional grilled meats at a classic Basque steakhouse
  • Ferry home Thursday from Puerto de Bilbao at Santurtzi
